### **Subject: New Trimester, New Toolkit: Strategic Management (Tri 2)**

Hi everyone,

Welcome to the **Strategic Management** course! 

As many of you know, I spent the last trimester teaching **Brand Management to our Tri 4 students**. Along with that transition, I’m pleased to announce that our **Brand Management Club** is evolving into the **Strategic Management Club + Brand Management Club**. 

While the club will bridge both worlds, our classroom focus this trimester for **Trimester 2** is strictly on **Strategic Management**. We are moving from consumer-facing identities to the "Big Picture"—competitive advantage, industry structures, and data-driven decision-making.

To help you build professional-grade strategic models (and move beyond basic slides), I recommend installing these five tools on your Linux systems. You can install them directly via your terminal:

#### **1. XMind (Brainstorming & Frameworks)**
The gold standard for mind mapping. Essential for building complex SWOT, PESTEL, and VRIO frameworks.
> `sudo snap install xmind`

#### **2. LibreOffice Draw (Strategy Canvases)**
Superior to PowerPoint for custom diagrams. Use this to map out "Blue Ocean" Strategy Canvases or value curves.
> `sudo apt install libreoffice-draw`

#### **3. R-Base (Data-Driven Strategy)**
For the analysts. Use this to look at industry trends and competitive clustering through a quantitative lens.
> `sudo apt install r-base`

#### **4. GnuCash (Financial Health Analysis)**
Strategy is grounded in numbers. GnuCash helps you analyze a competitor’s "Resource-Based View" (RBV) by deconstructing their financial health.
> `sudo apt install gnucash`

#### **5. Dia (Value Chain Modeling)**
A lightweight tool for formal process mapping. Perfect for visualizing a firm’s internal **Value Chain** (Inbound Logistics to Service).
> `sudo apt install dia`

**Next Steps:** Please try to have **XMind** and **LibreOffice Draw** installed by the end of Week 2, if you are interested in exploring these tools. 

See you in class!
